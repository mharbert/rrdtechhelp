A Teacher's Guide to Data-Driven Decisions

Using Performance Matters Reports to Understand and Support Student Learning

Which Report Fits Your Needs?

Each report offers a unique lens into student data. This chart compares the primary focus of each tool, from broad district-level views to detailed individual student analysis. Use it to quickly find the right report for your specific analytical goal.

1. Baseball Card (BBC) Report

Your tool for creating a customized, longitudinal view of student performance across multiple assessments and measures like grades, state tests, and more.

Guiding Question: How are my students growing over time? This report helps you track individual progress across key benchmarks throughout the year.

2. Comparative Results Report

Analyze and compare student performance on multiple tests, focusing specifically on standards and item types to pinpoint curriculum gaps.

Guiding Question: How did my class perform on this standard compared to the school or district? This helps identify areas where your instruction was particularly effective.

3. Scoreboard Report

Compare average assessment scores across different courses, teachers, and schools to identify successful instructional strategies and trends.

Guiding Question: Which instructional approaches are showing the most success? This report helps identify effective strategies by comparing performance across different groups.

4. Standards Report

Track how well your students are mastering specific academic standards throughout the year and create targeted support groups.

Guiding Question: What is the overall mastery level of my class for a key standard? This visual makes it easy to see the proficiency breakdown and plan next steps.

5. Student Detail Report

The all-in-one view of an individual student, consolidating demographics, test results, standards performance, interventions, and accommodations into one place.

6. Student Item Analysis

Conduct a deep-dive into a specific test, analyzing how students performed on every question to identify common misconceptions.

Guiding Question: Was there a common incorrect answer that points to a class-wide misunderstanding? This analysis helps refine future lessons.
